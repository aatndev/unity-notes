PHYSICS AND RIGID BODY
---------------------------

DIRECTION: Vector3 direction = ending position - starting position
Random.Value; returns a random float value between 0 and 1

FORCE
-----
RigidBodyObj.AddForce(direction * force, ForceMode.forceMode);
-> playerRB.AddForce(Vector3.up * jumpForce, ForceMode.Impulse)


| ForceMode        | Instant?  | Ignores Mass? | Common Use             |
-------------------------------------------------------------------------
| `Force`          | ❌ No     | ❌ No        | Smooth movement        |
| `Acceleration`   | ❌ No     | ✅ Yes       | Uniform acceleration   |
| `Impulse`        | ✅ Yes    | ❌ No        | Explosions, hits       |
| `VelocityChange` | ✅ Yes    | ✅ Yes       | Arcade-style knockback | ...table taken from chatgpt...


ROTATIONAL FORCE / TORQUE
-------------------------
RigidBodyObj.AddTorque(x, y, z, ForceMode.forceMode);
-> playerRB.AddForce(5, 5, 5, ForceMode.Impulse)
where the 5s or x, y and z are the torque applied to x, y, and z axis

Linear and Angular Velocity
---------------------------
-> playerRb.linearVelocity = Vector3.zero; 
-> playerRb.angularVelocity = Vector3.zero; //no velocity


ACCESSING MAGNITUDE & DIRECTION - .magnitude & .normalized
-----------------------------------------------------------
Vector3 lookDirection = (player.transform.position - transform.position).normalized; //only gives the direction

Vector3 lookDirection = (player.transform.position - transform.position).magnitude; //only gives the magnitude


DEFINING VECTORS IN 3D SPACE
----------------------------
Vector3.forward - getting direction of gameObject
otherGameObj.transform.forward - same as Vector3.forward but diff gameObj attached to the script

APPL: To get the pt of obj2 10 units away from an obj1 to the direction where obj1 is looking :-
obj2Pos = obj1.transform.position + obj1.transform.forward * 10f;
